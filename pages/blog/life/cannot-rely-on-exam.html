人生无法通过考研上岸 Life Cannot Be Saved Through Graduate School Exams

一、引言

当朋圈刷屏的"拟录取"九宫格被整齐划一的"体制内日常vlog"取代，当代年轻人似乎在用脚投票：考研考公才是人生的终极救赎。可这场集体狂欢背后，我们是否正在陷入一场自我构建的溺水叙事？

When WeChat Moments are flooded with uniform "institutional daily vlogs" replacing the once popular "proposed admission" grid posts, contemporary young people seem to be voting with their feet: graduate school exams and civil service exams are the ultimate redemption in life. But behind this collective celebration, are we falling into a self-constructed drowning narrative?

二、"上岸"的幻象

十年前我在老家茶馆听老爷子们侃大山，"上岸"特指失足妇女改行开服装店。如今这个词摇身一变，成了985学霸们的通关暗号。我表弟备战考研时床头贴着"不上岸毋宁死"，不知道的还以为他在拍《海王》续集。

Ten years ago, in my hometown teahouse, I heard old men chatting about how "coming ashore" specifically referred to fallen women changing careers to open clothing stores. Now this term has transformed into a password for top university students. My cousin had "rather die than not come ashore" posted at his bedside while preparing for graduate exams - those unaware might think he was filming a sequel to "Aquaman."

考研机构总爱给我们看逆袭剧本，却把群演们的辛酸剪得干干净净。去年研招报录比4:1的残酷现实，在他们嘴里变成了"四份努力换一份幸运"的鸡汤。网上那些光鲜的成功故事，就像美颜过度的自拍，把现实的坑坑洼洼都磨平了。不信你去B站搜"章北海"，看看有多少人把"official"拼成"offical"——这届网友的真实水平，可比营销号编的励志故事可爱多了。

Graduate exam coaching institutions love to show us comeback scripts, while editing out all the extras' hardships. Last year's brutal reality of a 4:1 application-to-admission ratio became "four portions of effort for one portion of luck" in their mouths. Those glossy success stories online are like overly beautified selfies, smoothing out all the bumps of reality. Don't believe it? Search "Zhang Beihai" on Bilibili and see how many people spell "official" as "offical" - the actual level of these netizens is much more endearing than the inspirational stories fabricated by marketing accounts.

三、学术现实

真正困在水下的，是那些把高考工厂生存法则刻进DNA的灵魂。去年在图书馆通宵区，我亲眼见证过研二师兄的魔幻日常：他给知网论文标注重点的模样，活脱脱像在刷《五年高考三年模拟》，荧光笔划过文献时的沙沙声，恍惚间让人听见高三晚自习的翻卷声。最绝的是他自创的"文献阅读法"——用中学时背文言文的抑扬顿挫朗读SCI论文，美其名曰"学术熏陶要从语感抓起"。

Those truly trapped underwater are souls with college entrance exam factory survival rules carved into their DNA. Last year in the library's overnight area, I witnessed the magical daily routine of a second-year graduate student: his way of highlighting key points in CNKI papers looked exactly like working through "Five Years of College Entrance Exams, Three Years of Simulations," the scratching sound of his highlighter across literature momentarily making one hear the sound of turning exam papers during high school evening self-study. Most remarkable was his self-created "literature reading method" - reading SCI papers with the same cadence used for memorizing classical Chinese in middle school, euphemistically called "academic cultivation must start with language sense."

导师的微信群简直成了赛博衡中，凌晨两点的消息提示像催命符。有次教授在群里甩了篇外文文献，五分钟内要求所有人语音汇报读后感。我邻座的博士师姐边敷着眼膜边开麦，发言结尾还不忘补句"感谢老师栽培"，熟练得让人心疼。这种24小时待命的状态，让学术民工们练就了条件反射般的生存技能——上厕所刷小论文，吃饭时听学术播客，连做梦都在修改参考文献格式。

The supervisor's WeChat group has become a cyber Hengshan Middle School, with 2 AM message notifications like death summons. Once a professor dropped a foreign literature piece in the group, requiring everyone to report their impressions via voice message within five minutes. My neighboring PhD senior sister opened her mic while applying eye masks, not forgetting to add "thank you for your cultivation, teacher" at the end of her speech - skilled in a way that makes one's heart ache. This 24-hour standby status has trained academic laborers with conditioned reflex survival skills - browsing papers while using the toilet, listening to academic podcasts while eating, even dreaming about modifying reference format.

某次帮导师去行政楼取材料，误入传说中的心理咨询预约大厅。好家伙，取号机前排队的阵仗堪比网红奶茶店，电子屏上的候诊号码已经排到三周后。穿格子衫的码农小哥在自助机前疯狂刷新，嘴里念叨着"这可是比抢周杰伦演唱会票还难"；扎双马尾的萌妹捧着《存在主义心理治疗》，书页间还夹着没做完的行测题。最魔幻的是咨询室传出的对话片段："老师，我文献综述写到第三版了，导师说像高考作文...""同学，我们先来谈谈你上次凌晨三点在朋友圈发李诞语录的事..."

Once while helping my supervisor fetch materials from the administration building, I accidentally entered the legendary psychological counseling appointment hall. Goodness, the queue at the number machine rivaled popular bubble tea shops, with appointment numbers on the electronic screen already scheduled three weeks later. A programmer in a plaid shirt frantically refreshed the self-service machine, muttering "this is harder than grabbing Jay Chou concert tickets"; a cute girl with twin tails held "Existential Psychotherapy," with unfinished civil service aptitude test questions tucked between the pages. Most surreal was a dialogue fragment from the counseling room: "Teacher, I've written the third version of my literature review, my supervisor says it reads like a college entrance exam essay..." "Student, let's first discuss your posting of Li Dan quotes on WeChat Moments at 3 AM..."

四、数字表演

这些年轻人白天在朋友圈晒着"沉浸式学习vlog"，咖啡杯旁总要"不经意"露出平板里的英文文献，配文是"今天也是被学术宠幸的一天"。可深夜的树洞微博却暴露了真相："蓝条见底，但不敢关掉文献管理器""在厕所隔间偷吃抗焦虑药时，听见隔壁在背考研政治"。这种精分现场，活像参加假面舞会的落水者——既要维持优雅的舞姿，又要偷偷把救生衣往礼服里塞。有个学妹的比喻特别精准："我们就像被编程的AI，明明系统都快崩溃了，还坚持在对话框里发送'加油鸭'的表情包。"

These young people share "immersive study vlogs" on WeChat Moments during the day, always "accidentally" showing English literature on their tablets next to coffee cups, captioned "another day blessed by academia." But their anonymous Weibo posts at midnight reveal the truth: "Battery at 1%, but don't dare close the reference manager" "While secretly taking anti-anxiety medication in a bathroom stall, I heard someone next door reciting graduate political theory." This schizophrenic scene is like a drowning person at a masquerade ball - needing to maintain elegant dance postures while secretly stuffing a life jacket into their formal wear. One junior student's metaphor was particularly accurate: "We're like programmed AI, obviously the system is about to crash, yet we persist in sending 'cheer up duck' stickers in the dialogue box."

五、另类成功故事

在深圳白石洲的握手楼夹缝里，阿珍的美甲店亮着粉紫色霓虹灯。这个江西姑娘左手握着光疗笔，右手刷着抖音直播教学，给客人指甲盖上雕出会动的玲娜贝儿。隔壁房产中介小哥来补钻时神秘兮兮透露："珍姐你这手艺，顶我开三单佣金。"她只是笑笑，把掉钻的波西米亚风美甲重新镶上施华洛世奇水钻——昨天刚给跨境电商老板娘做完两千八的定制款，转头就在业主群收了四个学徒。

In the crevices of handshake buildings in Shenzhen's Baishizhou, Ah Zhen's nail salon glows with pink-purple neon lights. This Jiangxi girl holds a UV light pen in her left hand while scrolling through Douyin teaching livestreams with her right, carving animated LinaBell designs on clients' nails. When the neighboring real estate agent came to fix his nail gems, he mysteriously revealed: "Sister Zhen, your craftsmanship is worth three of my commission deals." She just smiled, replacing fallen Swarovski crystals on a Bohemian-style nail design—yesterday she completed a 2,800 yuan custom set for a cross-border e-commerce boss's wife, then promptly recruited four apprentices from the property management group.

两千公里外的北京潘家园旧货市场，算命老张的摊位永远是最旺的"风水宝地"。他给互联网大厂P8算流年运势用Scrum框架解说，给海淀妈妈解学业卦象会同步推荐教辅书商微信。最绝的是摊位招牌："塔罗预测融资轮次，紫微斗数解析OKR"。靠着这门传统与现代交融的绝活，老张悄么声在燕郊置了两套小三居，微信签名挂着"互联网+易学开创者"。

Two thousand kilometers away at Beijing's Panjiayuan Antique Market, Old Zhang's fortune-telling stall is always the most prosperous "feng shui treasure spot." He explains annual fortune readings for P8-level internet company employees using Scrum frameworks, and simultaneously recommends educational book merchant WeChat contacts when interpreting academic hexagrams for Haidian mothers. Most remarkable is his stall sign: "Tarot Predictions for Financing Rounds, Purple Star Astrology Analysis of OKRs." Relying on this unique skill blending tradition with modernity, Old Zhang quietly acquired two three-bedroom apartments in Yanjiao, with his WeChat signature displaying "Internet + Divination Pioneer."

六、重新定义成功

这些游离在主流叙事之外的生存大师，正在重新定义什么叫做"上岸"。当清北博士挤破头争抢人大附中教职，当常青藤海归在街道办写公文，我们或许该清醒了：那些被万千学子视为诺亚方舟的"岸"，搞不好是另一些人正想驾船逃离的围城。

These survival masters operating outside mainstream narratives are redefining what it means to "come ashore." When Tsinghua and Peking University PhDs scramble for teaching positions at Renmin University Affiliated High School, when Ivy League returnees write documents at street offices, perhaps we should wake up: those "shores" viewed as Noah's Ark by thousands of students might well be besieged cities that others are trying to escape by boat.

上周陪母亲去早市，卖菜刘姐的档口总是热闹非凡。她削莴笋皮的手法堪比外科手术，菜刀翻飞间突然蹦出一句："我要有你们读书的能耐，早把这条街的铺面盘下来当包租婆咯！"这话混着莴笋叶的清香和鱼摊的腥气，竟透出几分禅意。旁边卖豆腐的王叔接茬："可不是嘛，我家那傻小子在互联网大厂天天'赋能''抓手'的，赚得还没我磨豆浆多。"

Last week, accompanying my mother to the morning market, Sister Liu's vegetable stall was always extraordinarily lively. Her technique of peeling lettuce stems compares to surgical precision, and amid the flying vegetable knife she suddenly blurted out: "If I had your studying abilities, I'd have bought up all the shops on this street to be a landlady long ago!" These words, mixed with the fresh scent of lettuce leaves and the fishy smell from the fish stall, somehow conveyed a sense of Zen. Uncle Wang selling tofu next door chimed in: "That's right, my foolish son at that big internet company talks about 'empowerment' and 'handles' all day, earning less than I do making soy milk."

这种民间智慧让我想起《是，大臣》里汉弗莱的经典分类：公务员分三种，一种负责制造忙碌假象，一种真正推动文件流转，第三种专门研究怎么让前两类人相安无事。这种三分法在职场生存指南里同样适用——用三成精力完成核心任务，三成时间经营领导幻觉，剩下四成全用来保护自己的发际线和甲状腺。

This folk wisdom reminds me of Humphrey's classic classification in "Yes, Minister": civil servants are divided into three types - one responsible for creating the illusion of busyness, one truly pushing document circulation, and a third specializing in how to keep the first two types getting along peacefully. This tripartite division works equally well in workplace survival guides - use 30% of your energy to complete core tasks, 30% of your time to maintain leadership's illusions, and the remaining 40% entirely to protect your hairline and thyroid.

毕竟真正的聪明人都明白，在马拉松式的人生赛道里，懂得控制配速的才能笑到最后。

After all, truly clever people understand that in the marathon-like race of life, those who know how to control their pace will laugh last.

考研辅导机构不会告诉你，人生是片没有彼岸的汪洋。那些被装订成册的成功学案例，不过是资本浪潮暂时托起的浪花。真正的救赎或许始于承认：我们从来都不是溺水者，又何须执着于虚构的"岸"？

Graduate exam coaching institutions won't tell you that life is an ocean without another shore. Those bound success study cases are merely waves temporarily lifted by capital tides. True redemption perhaps begins with acknowledging: we were never drowning, so why obsess over a fictional "shore"?

当放下对标准答案的执念，或许会发现，生而为人，我们本就是陆地生物。

When we let go of our obsession with standard answers, perhaps we'll discover that as human beings, we are inherently land creatures.

结语

考研辅导机构不会告诉你，人生是片没有彼岸的汪洋。那些被装订成册的成功学案例，不过是资本浪潮暂时托起的浪花。真正的救赎或许始于承认：我们从来都不是溺水者，又何须执着于虚构的"岸"？

Graduate exam coaching institutions won't tell you that life is an ocean without another shore. Those bound success study cases are merely waves temporarily lifted by capital tides. True redemption perhaps begins with acknowledging: we were never drowning, so why obsess over a fictional "shore"?

当放下对标准答案的执念，或许会发现，生而为人，我们本就是陆地生物。

When we let go of our obsession with standard answers, perhaps we'll discover that as human beings, we are inherently land creatures.
